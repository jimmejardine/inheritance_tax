# Poaching and Haven Politics: When Jurisdictions Court the Wealthy — and Whether Their Voters Agree

**Executive summary.** Some jurisdictions do not merely fail to tax the wealthy; they actively recruit them — Queensland in 1976–77, Texas and Florida today, Monaco, Dubai, the Italian flat tax, the Swiss forfait, and one Arctic municipality that tried to become "the Monaco of the North." This file taxonomises the poaching playbook, consolidates the design's defences against it, and then asks the question the flight literature skips: do the *citizens* of courting jurisdictions actually want the influx? The verified record runs both ways — Texans voted 74% to constitutionalise their haven status, while Zurich, Lisbon, and Madrid voters dismantled theirs — and the pattern in the splits is the causal engine behind the repricing timeline in [exit-options.md](exit-options.md): in democracies, courtship of *wealth* (as opposed to retirees or workers) is politically self-limiting on roughly a decade scale, because its fiscal gains are diffuse and its housing costs are concentrated.

## Headline findings

- **Poaching is real, instrumented, and mostly sub-national or micro-sovereign.** The instruments are preferential foreigner regimes (forfait, Italian flat tax, NHR), visa products (golden visas, UAE golden visa), state marketing (Bjelke-Petersen's explicit retiree courtship; Bø's mayor phoning the rich), and trust-law arbitrage (South Dakota; Madrid's bonifications) — inventory in [exit-options.md](exit-options.md) and the haven typology in [`../11-geographies/unlikely-and-havens.md`](../11-geographies/unlikely-and-havens.md).
- **The marquee modern case — California→Texas — is income-and-cost arbitrage, not inheritance-tax poaching.** California's net domestic outmigration roughly doubled in the pandemic (peaking near 300,000/year; 200,000 net in 2023) and, unusually, skewed high-income [1]; Oracle (Dec 2020), HPE (Dec 2020), and Tesla (Dec 2021) moved headquarters to Texas [2][3][4]. But neither Texas, Florida, *nor California* levies an estate or inheritance tax — the estate-tax variant of interstate poaching is the Moretti–Wilson billionaire finding, engaged in [evidence.md](evidence.md) §3, and both cases argue the same settled conclusion: national assignment ([`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md), error #5).
- **The strongest verified "citizens want it" datum:** Texas voters approved Proposition 4 in November 2019, constitutionally banning a state income tax, by 74% to 26% [5]; Florida's voters constitutionalised their income-and-inheritance-tax ban as early as 1924 [6][7]. Queensland's 1977 election returned the abolishing government with a commanding majority [8].
- **The strongest verified "citizens don't want it" data:** Zurich abolished the forfait by referendum in February 2009 (52.9%), and Appenzell Ausserrhoden, Basel-Landschaft, Basel-Stadt, and Schaffhausen followed [9]; Portugal ended the golden visa's real-estate route in the October 2023 Mais Habitação law after housing protests [11]; Spain abolished its golden visa outright from 3 April 2025, explicitly on housing grounds [12]; Norway's Bø experiment landed one major business family, one skiing legend, and a national row over who pays for the shortfall [15][16][17].
- **The split verdict is instructive:** Switzerland rejected *federal* forfait abolition 59.2% in 2014 even as individual cantons kept abolishing locally [9][10] — the costs of hosting are local and salient; the gains are thin and diffuse; where the vote is local and the influx prices housing, the haven loses.
- **Synthesis (labelled judgement, §3.3):** in franchised polities, wealth-courtship regimes survive about a decade before referendum or repeal; the durable havens are micro-states and non-democracies — precisely the capacity-bounded set of [exit-options.md](exit-options.md). Haven electorates are therefore future floor-coalition members, not permanent adversaries.

## 1. The poaching playbook, taxonomised

Two levels, four instruments.

**Sub-national courtship** operates inside a federation at near-zero migration cost: Queensland's 1976–77 death-duty abolition explicitly courting elderly southerners ([`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md); Pedrick's canonical account is that repo's source [7]); US states competing on income and estate taxes (the Moretti–Wilson margin, [evidence.md](evidence.md) §3); Madrid's 100% wealth-tax credit ([evidence.md](evidence.md) §4); South Dakota and Nevada selling perpetual-trust situs ([`../11-geographies/unlikely-and-havens.md`](../11-geographies/unlikely-and-havens.md), "internal havens"); and Bø kommune, which in 2021 became the first Norwegian municipality since 1978 to cut its wealth-tax share unilaterally [15]. **Sovereign courtship** is the haven inventory of [exit-options.md](exit-options.md) — Monaco's residence-by-deposit, Dubai's golden visa, the Italian flat tax, Portugal's NHR (†2024), the Swiss forfait — not re-inventoried here.

The instruments, wherever deployed:

| Instrument | Mechanism | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Preferential foreigner regimes | Tax the incomer more lightly than the native | Swiss forfait; Italian €200k flat tax; Portuguese NHR; UK non-dom (†2025) — all catalogued in [exit-options.md](exit-options.md) |
| Visa/citizenship products | Sell residence or passports against investment | Golden visas (Portugal, Spain, Cyprus, Malta); UAE golden visa; Caribbean CBI |
| State marketing | The jurisdiction advertises for the wealthy | Bjelke-Petersen's retiree campaign; Bø's mayor courting rich Norwegians through the press [16]; Dubai's relocation industry |
| Legal-wrapper arbitrage | Sell trust, foundation, or secrecy law rather than residence | South Dakota perpetuities; Liechtenstein anstalts; Jersey trusts ([`../11-geographies/unlikely-and-havens.md`](../11-geographies/unlikely-and-havens.md)) |

### The California→Texas case, at honest scale

The most-cited contemporary poaching story deserves precise numbers and an honest caveat. California's Legislative Analyst's Office, working from IRS migration data, records net domestic outmigration rising from ~170,000/year pre-pandemic to ~300,000/year in 2020–22, easing to 200,000 in 2023 (590,000 out, 390,000 in); unusually, the pandemic wave skewed *high-income* — outmigration peaked at 1.7% of taxpayers earning over $200k in 2022 — and forgone revenue peaked at 1.6% of income-tax receipts, with Texas the largest single income destination [1]. The corporate marquee matched the household flow: Oracle announced its HQ move to Austin on 11 December 2020 [2], HPE announced its move to the Houston area on 1–3 December 2020 [3], and Tesla completed its relocation to Austin on 1 December 2021 [4].

**The honest counterweight.** This is *income-and-cost* arbitrage — top-rate differentials, housing costs, remote work — not inheritance-tax poaching: Texas and Florida levy no estate or inheritance tax, *but neither does California*, so no death-tax differential exists on that route at all. The estate-tax variant of interstate poaching is precisely what Moretti & Wilson measured (35% of billionaires leaving estate-tax states — repo-verified in [evidence.md](evidence.md) §3), and even it left the taxing states revenue-positive. Both cases converge on the settled design conclusion of [`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md) (error #5): intra-federation flight is the zero-friction worst case, it is why bequest taxation must sit at national level at minimum, and it is *not* evidence about international elasticities ([evidence.md](evidence.md) §5). That question is settled in this repo and not re-litigated here.

## 2. Mitigations against active courtship

The design's defences against a poaching jurisdiction are already specified across this section; this consolidates them against the specific threat of a *recruiting* competitor rather than a passive one.

| Defence | Against what | Where specified |
|---|---|---|
| **National assignment** | The Queensland cascade — sub-national defection is costless, so never assign the tax below national level | [`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md) error #5 |
| **Dual nexus / situs** | The poacher recruits the *person*; UK land, registered shares, and operating businesses stay in charge whoever leaves — a foreign-resident dynasty warehouses UK assets no better than a domestic one | [`../02-proposal/avoidance.md`](../02-proposal/avoidance.md) §5; [`../16-second-order/foreign-ownership.md`](../16-second-order/foreign-ownership.md) §1 |
| **The 10-year tail** | The recruit stays in the former home state's net for a decade — CJEU-blessed (*van Hilten*), so even an EU poacher cannot offer a clean exit | [exit-tax-design.md](exit-tax-design.md); [international-coordination.md](international-coordination.md) |
| **UTPR-analogue defensive measures** | A floor coalition tops up what the poacher undertaxes, converting recruitment into a transfer *from* the poacher's treasury | [international-coordination.md](international-coordination.md) |
| **Congestion pricing** | The poacher's own housing market and entry rationing cap the intake — flight is self-limiting in aggregate | [exit-options.md](exit-options.md) §2; [game-theory.md](game-theory.md), small-coalition theorem |
| **Clean hands** | The design's own posture is *neutral admission — no courting, no exclusion* ([inbound-wealth.md](inbound-wealth.md) §7): it never plays the poaching game, so it never inherits the golden-visa record or the housing backlash documented in §3 below | [inbound-wealth.md](inbound-wealth.md) |

The contrast in the last row is worth making explicit, because it is a campaign asset: every poaching instrument in §1 eventually forced its jurisdiction to choose between its wealthy incomers and its own electorate (§3). A regime that admits wealth neutrally, discloses it at entry, and harvests it at transfer never faces that choice — the flypaper thesis of [inbound-wealth.md](inbound-wealth.md) §2 is the anti-poaching stance.

**What is genuinely not mitigable unilaterally.** None of these tools reaches the *formation margin*: the young founder who builds the fortune in the poacher's jurisdiction from day one, is never resident, and never owns domestic-situs assets. That is the honest residual of [game-theory.md](game-theory.md) — a multi-decade erosion risk run by a rule-of-law haven capturing company formation, bounded by agglomeration economics ([retention-attraction.md](retention-attraction.md)) but not closed by any exit architecture, and answerable only by the floor coalition.

## 3. Do the courting jurisdiction's citizens want it? The verified record

The flight debate treats destination jurisdictions as unitary actors. They are not: courtship is chosen by governments, and its costs — housing above all — land on voters. The record runs both directions, and the direction tracks *who arrives*.

### 3.1 Yes: when the influx is retirees and there is no price shock

- **Queensland, 1977.** Bjelke-Petersen abolished death duties effective 1 January 1977, explicitly courting elderly interstate migrants (repo-verified via Pedrick in [`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md)). At the first post-abolition election, 12 November 1977, his coalition was returned for a fourth consecutive term with a commanding majority — Nationals 35, Liberals 24, Labor 23 of 82 seats, the Nationals outpolling the Liberals for the first time in Queensland history [8]. Honest attribution: the campaign was dominated by law-and-order, Labor actually recovered 12 seats from its 1974 wipeout, and a favourable redistribution helped — so 1977 is evidence that abolition-plus-courtship *cost nothing and coexisted with victory*, not a measured mandate for it. But the retiree influx became part of Queensland's durable growth identity, and no subsequent government paid a price for the abolition. Courtship *can* be a lasting electoral winner — when the recruits are sunbelt retirees arriving at housing-stock scale over decades, not price-shock wealth arriving into a fixed housing stock.
- **Texas, 2019.** The strongest verifiable "citizens vote for haven status" datum anywhere: Proposition 4, constitutionally prohibiting an individual income tax, approved 74%–26% in November 2019 [5]. **Florida** did the equivalent a century earlier: a 1924 legislatively referred amendment prohibiting both income and inheritance taxes, still embedded as Article VII §5 of the state constitution [6][7]. Low-tax identity in both states is not an elite imposition; it is civic self-definition, ratified at the ballot — which is exactly why [evidence.md](evidence.md)'s Florida-specific pull is durable and why the design treats US non-adoption as a scenario, not a surprise ([`../11-geographies/unlikely-and-havens.md`](../11-geographies/unlikely-and-havens.md)).

### 3.2 No: the backlash record

- **Switzerland.** Zurich voters abolished the forfait on 8 February 2009 by 52.9%, on a fairness campaign — identical residents, different tax bills; 97 of the canton's 201 forfait holders left [9]. Schaffhausen, Appenzell Ausserrhoden, Basel-Landschaft, and Basel-Stadt abolished it too, while other cantons kept it with tightened minimums [9]. The 2014 federal initiative to abolish it nationwide lost 59.2%–40.8%, with only Schaffhausen in favour [9][10]. Read the split carefully: where the wealthy foreigners actually live (Zurich, Basel), voters abolished; the nation, voting on other cantons' revenue, kept it. Hosting costs are local; that is where the votes turn.
- **Portugal.** The Lisbon/Porto housing crisis — prices up 55% in a decade against 9% income growth — produced 2023's mass housing protests and the *Mais Habitação* law (approved by the Assembly in September 2023 over a presidential veto, enacted October 2023), which killed the golden visa's real-estate route, previously ~75% of applications [11]; NHR closed to new entrants from 2024 (repo-verified — [exit-options.md](exit-options.md) [8]). Fourteen years from launch to repeal.
- **Spain.** Sánchez announced abolition of the golden visa in April 2024 framed explicitly as housing policy — housing "a fundamental right, not a speculative business," against a documented surge in non-resident foreign purchases; Organic Law 1/2025 ended the programme definitively on 3 April 2025 [12].
- **Singapore** never waited for the backlash: the 60% ABSD on foreign residential purchases (April 2023) is pre-emptive self-restraint by the model haven (repo-verified — [exit-options.md](exit-options.md) [7]).
- **Miami and Austin** are the same politics in pre-referendum form, *with the causality label attached: contested*. Miami sits at or near the bottom of every US rental-affordability ranking — Redfin's December 2024 data show the typical Miami renter household earning ~40% less ($57,157) than the $94,920 needed to afford the median asking rent [13]. Austin's pandemic boom produced a severe 2021–22 rent spike, then the instructive counter-case: a permitting surge (957 apartments per 100,000 residents in 2021–23, roughly triple peer metros) drove 19 consecutive months of rent declines from the 2022 peak, though rents remain ~17% above pre-pandemic and nearly half of renters cost-burdened [14]. How much of the affordability squeeze is attributable to wealthy in-migration specifically, versus general population growth and supply constraints, is genuinely unsettled (§4) — but the *politics* does not wait for the econometrics, which is the point of this section.
- **Norway's Bø kommune, the miniature.** In 2021 Bø (pop. ~2,700) cut its municipal wealth-tax share, taking the local rate from 0.85% to 0.35% — "Monaco of the North" [15][16]. The measured result: 56 wealth-tax payers moved in over 2020–22 (against ≤6/year before), average taxable wealth rose 60%, and the arrivals included the Adolfsen brothers' hotel-and-care empire and skiing legend Bjørn Dæhlie [15][17]. But Norway's tax-equalisation system meant Bø did not keep the revenue it forwent — the cut opened an unforeseen budget gap, the Conservative government's decision to help cover it in the 2021 budget enraged the left ("ordinary Norwegians paying for Bø's gamble"), and the episode became a national argument about municipalities defecting at everyone else's expense [16]. One municipality can defect; it cannot make the rest of the country subsidise the defection quietly.

### 3.3 Synthesis — labelled judgement

The pattern across §3.1–3.2 is consistent enough to state as this file's central (judgement) claim: **in democracies, courting mobile wealth is politically self-limiting, on roughly a decade scale.** The fiscal gains are real but diffuse — a thin revenue stream, some consumption; the costs are concentrated and visible — housing above all, plus the fairness optics of taxing incomers more lightly than natives. Concentrated costs beat diffuse gains in referendum politics: hence Zurich 2009, NHR 2023, Spain 2025, Singapore's pre-emption, and Bø's national row — this is the causal engine that generates the uniform direction of the repricing table in [exit-options.md](exit-options.md). The exceptions prove the mechanism: courtship survives where the influx is *retirees absorbed at housing-stock scale* (Queensland, Florida — the sunbelt model, whose "recruits" are the settling middle class, not price-shock fortunes) or where *there is no franchise to object* (Monaco's electorate is a fraction of its residents; Dubai's non-citizen majority has none). The durable havens are therefore micro-states and non-democracies — exactly the capacity-bounded, reputationally ceilinged set of [exit-options.md](exit-options.md) and [`../11-geographies/unlikely-and-havens.md`](../11-geographies/unlikely-and-havens.md). Strategic consequence: the electorates of would-be poachers are not the floor coalition's enemies but its future members — Lisbon's renters and Zurich's referendum majority want the same thing the floor delivers, and coalition diplomacy ([international-coordination.md](international-coordination.md)) should treat haven *publics* and haven *governments* as separate audiences.

## 4. Honest limits

- **The UAE counterexample.** Dubai absorbed the post-2022 Russian wealth influx at scale and thrived — grey-listed, then tightened AML and exited the FATF list in 2024 ([`../11-geographies/unlikely-and-havens.md`](../11-geographies/unlikely-and-havens.md) [7][8]). The §3.3 mechanism runs through voters; where the priced-out majority has no franchise, it does not run. The self-limiting claim is a claim about democracies, and the largest-capacity modern haven is not one.
- **Queensland shows durable-winner courtship exists.** The 1977 verdict was never reversed, and no Australian government has reinstated death duties. Where the courted population is retirees arriving over decades into an expanding housing stock, the political ledger can stay positive indefinitely. The self-limiting pattern is about *wealth-shock* courtship, not all courtship.
- **Wealth-migration→housing causality is contested.** Serious causal work exists — Sá finds foreign investment raised English and Welsh house prices materially (average prices ~19% lower in 2014 absent it, on an instrumented design) [18], and golden-visa studies find measurable price premia at investment thresholds in Portugal [19] — but disentangling wealthy in-migration from generic demand growth and supply constraint is unresolved, and Austin's supply-driven correction [14] shows the cost side is at least partly a policy choice of the destination. This file's §3 claims are about the *politics* of perceived causation, which moves on salience, not identification strategies.
- **Texas's pull is substantially jobs and housing supply, not just tax.** The LAO data show cost-of-living and remote work driving the outflow [1]; Austin permits housing at triple peer rates [14]. Reading California→Texas as pure tax competition flatters the poaching narrative; reading it as *cost* competition is closer to the evidence, and cost competition is one the taxing jurisdiction can answer with supply policy rather than rate surrender.
- **Verification residuals.** The 1977 Queensland result is verified but the abolition's *contribution* to it is attribution by circumstance, not polling; Bø's long-run fiscal ledger (post-2022) remains unpublished; and no named billionaire "Wiese" could be verified in the Bø episode — the verified movers are the Adolfsen family and Dæhlie [15][17].

## Research gaps

- Queensland 1977: locate contemporary polling or press analysis isolating death-duty abolition's electoral salience (Pedrick covers the policy, not the vote).
- Bø kommune: obtain the municipality's post-2022 accounts and the final equalisation treatment — the definitive small-N test of whether sub-national defection pays after clawback.
- A comparative dataset of haven-regime lifespans (launch→repeal/repricing) to test the "decade scale" judgement in §3.3 quantitatively.
- Track Miami-Dade and Austin municipal politics for the first US referendum-grade backlash against wealth in-migration (candidate measures: vacancy taxes, non-resident surcharges).
