# Pilots: What Can and Cannot Be Tested First

**Executive summary.** The tax half of the scheme cannot be piloted — sub-national trial runs are self-destroying (Australia's 1977–79 cascade is the proof) and a "pilot" announced nationally is just an announcement with extra forestalling time — but the endowment half can be, and has been: the UK Child Trust Fund, US baby-bond programmes, and lottery-windfall natural experiments together form a usable evidence base and, more importantly, a template for pilots that build a constituency rather than merely a dataset.

## Why the tax cannot be piloted

| Pilot form | Why it fails |
|---|---|
| Sub-national (one state/region trials the tax) | Wealth and residents relocate to the untaxed jurisdictions; the pilot jurisdiction "proves" capital flight and the idea dies. Australia 1977–79 is the canonical cascade: one state's defection unravelled every state *and* the federal duty within three years ([`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md)) |
| Temporal (trial for five years, then review) | A known sunset is an invitation to defer every taxable event past it — the inverse of the forestalling problem ([announcement-effects.md](announcement-effects.md)); the 2001–2010 US sunset farce shows how expiry dates corrupt behaviour ([legislative-path-us.md](legislative-path-us.md)) |
| Demographic (apply only to estates above £10m "to start") | This is not a pilot; it is Phase 2 of the sequencing ladder ([sequencing.md](sequencing.md)) and should be owned as such |
| Voluntary / shadow (compute liabilities without collecting) | Useful — this is essentially what Phase 0's receipts register does: dry-running the ledger on real probate data before any rate attaches ([`../02-proposal/mechanism.md`](../02-proposal/mechanism.md) §6) |

The honest position: for the tax, the "pilots" are (i) the shadow ledger, (ii) other countries' operating regimes — Ireland's CAT is a four-decade live trial of recipient-based taxation ([`../11-geographies/ireland.md`](../11-geographies/ireland.md)) — and (iii) the earlier rungs of the ladder itself, each of which generates data for the next.

## The endowment can be piloted — and has been

### UK Child Trust Fund (2005–2011)

The CTF gave every child born from September 2002 a government-seeded account (£250 at birth, £250 at seven, more for low-income families), locked until 18. Its lessons are the closest thing to a domestic endowment trial:

- **Design lesson — engagement decays.** A large share of accounts became effectively dormant; at maturity (from September 2020) very large numbers went unclaimed — the NAO found 45% of the first tranche of matured accounts (145,000 of 320,000, holding £394m) unclaimed by April 2021 [2], and by April 2025 around 758,000 matured accounts holding over £1.5bn (average £2,242) remained unclaimed [1]. An endowment paid at 25 as a single visible event, claimed through an active process, avoids the drip-and-forget failure; this is part of the age-25 rationale in [`../02-proposal/endowment.md`](../02-proposal/endowment.md).
- **Political lesson — small and future benefits die quietly.** The CTF was cancelled in 2010 with minimal outcry: sums were modest, beneficiaries were children (non-voters), and the first payout was a decade away. No constituency existed to defend it. This is the abolition-pattern warning ([`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md), error #6) applied to the *benefit* side: an endowment must reach identifiable adults early enough, and visibly enough, that cancelling it has a face.
- **Evidence lesson.** The CTF cohort provides a natural experiment on asset-holding effects at 18, still under-exploited in the literature (unverified).

### US baby bonds

- **Connecticut Baby Bonds** (enacted 2021, operating since 1 July 2023): $3,200 invested at birth for each HUSKY (Medicaid)-covered birth, enrolled automatically, claimable at ages 18–30 for wealth-building uses and projected to be worth roughly $11,000–$24,000 at claim — the first state-scale programme in operation [3].
- **DC Child Wealth Building Act** (2021): a $500 initial deposit plus $600–$1,000 a year for children born to households under 300% of the federal poverty level [4], projected to reach roughly $21,000 at 18 for the poorest children [5] — but implementation was chronically delayed and the Act was repealed outright in DC's FY2026 budget, effective 30 September 2025 [6]: a live demonstration of the political lesson above.
- **Federal proposals**: Booker–Pressley American Opportunity Accounts ($1,000 at birth plus income-scaled annual deposits, reaching an estimated $46,215 at 18 for the lowest-income children [7]) — repeatedly introduced, not passed; useful as costed legislative drafting.

These are *targeted* (progressive by family income) rather than universal, and small relative to £50k. They test delivery machinery, take-up, and use-patterns — not the full social meaning of a universal endowment. The universality argument is made in [`../02-proposal/endowment.md`](../02-proposal/endowment.md).

### Natural experiments: windfalls at scale

The behavioural questions — does a young adult given a five-figure sum work less, waste it, or invest it? — are answerable now from lottery and inheritance natural experiments: Swedish lottery winners reduce earnings immediately, persistently and modestly — a lifetime marginal propensity to earn out of unearned income of about −0.11, with no mass exit from work [8] — and Dutch winners cut hours without leaving employment [9]; UK premium-bond and inheritance-receipt studies and Georgia (US) HOPE-scholarship-style cohort designs extend the picture (specific findings unverified). This literature is assembled in [`../16-second-order/endowment-effects.md`](../16-second-order/endowment-effects.md) and answers the "they'll blow it all" objection in [`../09-objections/spend-it-all.md`](../09-objections/spend-it-all.md).

## Pilot design that builds constituency, not just evidence

The CTF's cancellation teaches that a pilot optimised purely for clean evaluation (small sums, long horizons, quiet administration) is politically disposable. A transition-serving endowment pilot should be engineered for *both* evidence and defence:

| Design choice | Evidence-only pilot | Constituency-building pilot |
|---|---|---|
| Cohort | Random sample, geographically scattered | Full age-cohorts in named places (every 25-year-old in two or three towns) — creates visible, interviewable, organisable beneficiaries |
| Sum | Small (£1–5k) for budget reasons | Meaningful fraction of the real endowment (£10–20k) so effects and testimony are real |
| Horizon | Payout years away | Payment within the pilot parliament, so results and faces exist before the next election |
| Funding narrative | General revenue | Explicitly linked to inheritance-tax receipts, rehearsing the hypothecation story ([`../02-proposal/endowment.md`](../02-proposal/endowment.md)) |
| Evaluation | RCT purity | RCT where possible, but saturation sites accepted — the comparison towns are the control |

A saturated-place pilot ("every 25-year-old in Grimsby and Merthyr") produces local economic effects, local press, and local defenders — the constituency mechanics belong to [`../13-movement/recruitment.md`](../13-movement/recruitment.md) and the messaging to [`../14-communications/audience-messaging.md`](../14-communications/audience-messaging.md) — while still generating cohort-comparison evidence.

## What the pilots feed

- **Delivery machinery** for Phase 3's national launch ([sequencing.md](sequencing.md)): identity verification, payment rails, guidance provision, fraud controls ([`../07-implementation/administration.md`](../07-implementation/administration.md)).
- **Revenue-bridge calibration**: observed claim rates and timing feed the cashflow model in [revenue-bridge.md](revenue-bridge.md).
- **The campaign**: beneficiary testimony is the single most valuable communications asset the movement can manufacture ([`../14-communications/narratives.md`](../14-communications/narratives.md)).

The asymmetry is the point: the tax must be argued from history and design; the endowment can be *demonstrated*. Lead the transition's public phase with the demonstrable half.

## What pilots cannot settle

Honesty about the limits keeps the evidence claims defensible:

- **General-equilibrium effects.** A pilot cannot reveal what happens to asset prices, savings rates, or giving when the *whole economy* faces the regime — those questions belong to modelling and cross-country comparison ([`../16-second-order/asset-prices.md`](../16-second-order/asset-prices.md), [`../16-second-order/macro.md`](../16-second-order/macro.md)).
- **Avoidance-industry response.** Planners do not mobilise against a pilot; the real stress test of the anti-avoidance spine only comes at national commencement ([`../02-proposal/avoidance.md`](../02-proposal/avoidance.md)).
- **The social meaning of universality.** A targeted or place-limited grant is experienced as welfare; a universal endowment is experienced as citizenship. Pilot evidence on stigma and take-up therefore *understates* the universal scheme's performance — an asymmetry worth stating in advance rather than conceding under attack ([`../09-objections/practicality.md`](../09-objections/practicality.md)).
