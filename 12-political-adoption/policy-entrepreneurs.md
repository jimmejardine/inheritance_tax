# Policy Entrepreneurs: Streams, Pipelines, and Laundering Machines

**Executive summary.** Ideas do not walk into manifestos; they are carried by policy entrepreneurs through identifiable institutional channels — think-tanks, special advisers, commissions, parliamentary devices, and citizens' assemblies — and they land when John Kingdon's three streams (problem, policy, politics) align in a window. This file maps those channels for the receipts tax and endowment, identifies the gatekeepers at each, and specifies what the movement must have pre-positioned before a window opens.

## Kingdon's multiple-streams framework

Kingdon's model (from *Agendas, Alternatives, and Public Policies*, 1984 — to verify edition details) holds that adoption requires three independently flowing streams to couple:

| Stream | Content | State for this policy | Movement task |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Problem** | A condition recognised as a problem demanding government action | Strong and strengthening: housing unaffordability, generational unfairness, fiscal gaps ([../01-problem/README.md](../01-problem/README.md)) | Keep indicators and focusing events attached to *inheritance* specifically, not just "inequality" |
| **Policy** | A worked-out, technically credible proposal "softened up" in the expert community | Partially built: Mirrlees-style receipts taxation has expert respectability; the full endowment pairing does not yet | Complete the softening-up: costings, drafting, journal and think-tank treatments ([../02-proposal/README.md](../02-proposal/README.md) is the seed) |
| **Politics** | Partisan advantage, public mood, coalition arithmetic | Latent: the self-interested routes exist ([left-parties.md](left-parties.md), [right-parties.md](right-parties.md), [centrists-and-liberals.md](centrists-and-liberals.md)) but no party has priced them | Brief each party's strategists in their own goal-language ([party-incentives.md](party-incentives.md)) |

**Windows** open when streams couple — typically at fiscal crises, post-defeat party reviews, leadership changes, and coalition negotiations ([../11-geographies/crisis-windows.md](../11-geographies/crisis-windows.md)). Kingdon's core discipline: windows are short and cannot be scheduled, so the entrepreneur's job is to have the proposal *complete and waiting* before the window, not to start drafting when it opens. Everything below is pre-positioning.

## The think-tank-to-manifesto pipeline

The standard UK route by which an outsider idea becomes a manifesto line, with its gatekeepers:

| Stage | Vehicle | Gatekeeper | What they need to say yes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Respectability | Academic papers; IFS/Resolution Foundation-tier analysis | Editors, institute directors | Technical novelty and defensible numbers, not advocacy |
| 2. Translation | Think-tank report with costings and polling (IPPR/Fabians for left; Onward/CPS-type for right; SMF/Institute-neutral for centre) | Report commissioners, funders | A fundable, newsworthy angle fitting the tank's brand |
| 3. Circulation | Pamphlets, conference fringe events, columnists | Comment editors, event programmers | A named author with standing; a counter-intuitive hook ("the conservative case for…") |
| 4. Party digestion | Party policy commissions, affiliated groups, frontbench teams | Special advisers, policy directors | Attack-survivability and a beneficiary map ([party-incentives.md](party-incentives.md)) |
| 5. Commitment | Manifesto | Leader's office, manifesto coordinator | Low fiscal-scoring risk, one-sentence retail version |

Two practical notes. First, the pipeline is *duplicated per party family*: the same policy needs a left-tank treatment (funding the base's programme), a right-tank treatment (property-owning democracy, tax cuts), and a liberal treatment (Mill, opportunity) — written in different vocabularies by different authors ([../14-communications/audience-messaging.md](../14-communications/audience-messaging.md)). Second, stage 1 is already partially purchased: the Mirrlees Review's sympathetic treatment of lifetime-receipts taxation (to verify exact recommendation wording) means the idea enters the pipeline with IFS-adjacent respectability rather than from zero.

## Special advisers: the real adoption surface

Formal party structures are not where adoption happens. In the UK system, the effective decision surface is a few dozen special advisers (SpAds), policy-unit staff, and shadow-team researchers who control what reaches a frontbencher's desk and in what frame. Their incentive structure is personal and short-horizon: they want ideas that make their principal look good at the next fiscal event, survive a hostile interview, and carry no career-ending risk.

Consequences for the movement:

- **Products, not arguments.** The SpAd-facing deliverable is a two-page note with costings, attack rebuttals, and a draft announcement — not a manifesto. The 80-page case exists to make the two-pager credible.
- **Career-arc targeting.** Today's opposition researcher is next parliament's Treasury adviser. Relationships built when a party is out of power (and hungry for ideas) mature exactly when they are needed ([../13-movement/recruitment.md](../13-movement/recruitment.md)).
- **The deniability requirement.** Advisers adopt ideas they can present as their principal's own. The movement should optimise for *uncredited* adoption — influence measured in policy text, not acknowledgements.

## Commissions and reviews: laundering machines for radical ideas

An idea too radical for a politician to voice becomes discussable the moment a grey-haired commission recommends a version of it. The mechanism is reputational transfer: the commission's independence and expertise substitute for the politician's courage. Precedents:

- **The Mirrlees Review (IFS, 2010–11)** — the canonical case for this policy area: an independent, prestigious tax review that recommended moving toward lifetime-receipts taxation of transfers (to verify), giving every subsequent proponent a citation that is not a politician or activist.
- **Royal Commissions and official reviews** (Turner on pensions is the modern UK exemplar — to verify) — capable of converting a third-rail topic into a phased consensus programme.
- **Party-commissioned reviews in opposition** — the standard vehicle for rung 2 of the adoption ladder ([party-incentives.md](party-incentives.md)): the leadership gets optionality ("we will study the review's findings") while the idea acquires internal legitimacy.

Movement implication: one strategic objective should be the commissioning — by a government, a party, or a credible foundation — of an independent review of wealth-transfer taxation with terms of reference that include recipient-based and endowment options. Writing the terms of reference is the highest-leverage document in this entire file.

## Parliamentary devices: salience tools

Private members' bills, ten-minute-rule bills, opposition-day debates, and select-committee inquiries almost never legislate, and that is not their function. Their function is to (a) force the question onto the record and make every MP take a position — raw material for the wedge plays in [wedge-dynamics.md](wedge-dynamics.md); (b) generate a drafting artefact (a bill text that exists and can be re-introduced); (c) give the media a peg; and (d) identify the sympathetic backbenchers who become the internal caucus. A single backbencher at rung 1 of the ladder is cheap to recruit and sufficient to start; select-committee inquiries (Treasury Committee on IHT reliefs, for instance) additionally compel evidence from HMRC and lobbyists that becomes ammunition ([../10-opposition/astroturf-and-lobbying.md](../10-opposition/astroturf-and-lobbying.md)).

## Citizens' assemblies: the legitimation route for third-rail topics

Ireland's Citizens' Assembly preceded and enabled the abortion (2018) and earlier the marriage-equality (2015) referendums — issues every Irish party had judged too dangerous to lead on (to verify institutional sequence). The mechanism maps precisely onto inheritance taxation, which shares the defining features: high emotional charge, entrenched elite framing, and party-strategic paralysis despite shifting public opinion.

Why the assembly route suits this policy specifically:

1. **Deliberation moves people on exactly this issue.** Inheritance-tax opinion is famously unstable under information — support rises when respondents learn actual thresholds and incidence (to verify against deliberative-polling literature; [../06-palatability/polling.md](../06-palatability/polling.md)). An assembly is institutionalised information exposure.
2. **It de-risks the politicians.** "We are implementing the assembly's recommendation" is the strongest available deniability wrapper — stronger than a commission, because the authors are demonstrably ordinary voters, not experts or activists.
3. **It pre-empts the astroturf counter-narrative.** Opposition claims that the policy is an elite/activist imposition ([../10-opposition/media-narratives.md](../10-opposition/media-narratives.md)) collapse against a random-sample citizen mandate.

The movement's play: campaign for an assembly on "intergenerational fairness and the taxation of wealth transfers" — a framing broad enough to commission, specific enough that the receipts/endowment design is squarely within scope ([../13-movement/theory-of-change.md](../13-movement/theory-of-change.md)).

## Pre-positioning checklist

What must exist *before* a window opens, per Kingdon's discipline:

- Draft legislation and administrative design ([../02-proposal/mechanism.md](../02-proposal/mechanism.md), [../07-implementation/administration.md](../07-implementation/administration.md))
- Independent costings robust to Treasury/OBR-grade scrutiny ([../02-proposal/thresholds.md](../02-proposal/thresholds.md))
- Party-dialect briefings for all three families (this section, files 4–6)
- Attack rebuttal toolkit ([../14-communications/inoculation.md](../14-communications/inoculation.md), [../09-objections/README.md](../09-objections/README.md))
- Named validators: economists, farmers, founders, above-threshold endorsers ([../13-movement/messengers.md](../13-movement/messengers.md))
- A stepping-stone menu scaled to window size ([stepping-stones.md](stepping-stones.md)) — small window, small step; crisis window, full scheme

## Bottom line

Adoption is a logistics problem before it is a persuasion problem. The channels are known, the gatekeepers are identifiable, and every one of them responds to pre-fabricated, risk-priced product rather than moral argument. The movement that has the two-pager, the bill text, the costings, the review terms of reference, and the assembly campaign ready — and waits — will look lucky when the window opens.
