# Crisis Windows: When Adoption Becomes Possible

**Executive summary.** The historical record — formalised in Walter Scheidel's *The Great Leveler* — is blunt: large compressions of wealth inequality have come almost exclusively through shocks (mass-mobilisation war, revolution, state collapse, plague), and the existing estate taxes of the twentieth century were substantially war-finance creations. The strategic conclusion is not to wait for catastrophe but to apply Milton Friedman's dictum — "when that crisis occurs, the actions that are taken depend on the ideas that are lying around" — by having the fully drafted, objection-hardened design of [`../02-proposal/`](../02-proposal/README.md) ready before any of the five identifiable coming windows opens.

## The Scheidel constraint

*The Great Leveler* (2017) surveys inequality across recorded history and finds peacetime politics has rarely, if ever, produced major levelling; the "Four Horsemen" — mass-mobilisation warfare, transformative revolution, state failure, and lethal pandemics — did. The 1914–1945 shock era produced both the great twentieth-century compression and the high-water estate taxes: the UK's Estate Duty ratchets tracked the World Wars and their aftermath ([`../05-history/uk-estate-duty.md`](../05-history/uk-estate-duty.md)); the US federal estate tax was enacted in 1916 amid war-preparedness finance and peaked during WWII mobilisation ([`../05-history/us-estate-tax.md`](../05-history/us-estate-tax.md)). Mass-mobilisation war created both the fiscal need and — crucially — the moral claim: conscription of men legitimised conscription of wealth.

Taken neat, Scheidel is an argument against this whole project. Three qualifications keep it a constraint rather than a verdict:

1. **The mechanism is fiscal-political, not mystical.** Shocks work by creating revenue need, breaking status-quo coalitions, and licensing previously unthinkable ideas. Anything that reproduces those three conditions — not only the Four Horsemen — can open a window. COVID-19 demonstrated that (deficit-financed) trillion-scale fiscal improvisation is available to modern states within weeks.
2. **Scheidel measures outcomes, not attempts.** Institutions built in windows (NHS 1948, social security systems) persisted for decades of peace. The task is to *build in* the window and *entrench* after it — the endowment hypothecation is the entrenchment device ([`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md)).
3. **Policy variation within peacetime is real.** Japan broadened its inheritance-tax base in 2015; the UK legislated APR/BPR narrowing in 2024 ([korea-japan.md](korea-japan.md), [uk.md](uk.md)). Peacetime moves are smaller, but they compound.

## Candidate windows, ranked by expected arrival and usability

| # | Window | Mechanism | Timing | Usability for this proposal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Great wealth transfer visibility** | Estimated $80–100tn+ passing between generations in the US alone through ~2045–48, with UK estimates in the several-trillion-pound range (both to verify — Cerulli and UK wealth-survey figures) makes inheritance *the* economic story of the 2030s | Underway; salience rising | High: it is the proposal's own subject matter; every headline about heirs is free advertising ([`../06-palatability/framing.md`](../06-palatability/framing.md)) |
| 2 | **Housing-generation electoral tipping point** | The cohort locked out of ownership becomes the median voter; renter-majority electorates in major cities already ([`../01-problem/housing.md`](../01-problem/housing.md), [`../12-political-adoption/electoral-arithmetic.md`](../12-political-adoption/electoral-arithmetic.md)) | 2028–2040 by country | High: the endowment is a direct offer to this bloc |
| 3 | **Ageing-society fiscal crunch** | Pension/health costs force every treasury to find large non-labour tax bases; inheritance flows are the least-distorting candidate ([`../03-case-for/economic-efficiency.md`](../03-case-for/economic-efficiency.md)) | Chronic, intensifying through 2040s; Japan and Korea first ([korea-japan.md](korea-japan.md)) | Medium-high: pushes rate/base upward but competes with the endowment for the revenue |
| 4 | **Post-crisis fiscal consolidation** | Financial crisis, pandemic, or war-adjacent shock leaves debt overhang; "broadest shoulders" politics returns as after 1918/1945/2008 | Unschedulable; base rate roughly once a decade | High but brief: windows measured in months; requires oven-ready drafting ([`../15-transition/sequencing.md`](../15-transition/sequencing.md)) |
| 5 | **AI-driven capital-share shock** | If AI shifts income from labour to capital owners at scale, the legitimacy of inherited capital collapses while the fiscal base migrates to exactly what this tax reaches; a generalised version of Piketty's r > g ([`../03-case-for/piketty-r-vs-g.md`](../03-case-for/piketty-r-vs-g.md)) | Speculative; 2030s if at all | Potentially transformative: converts the proposal from redistribution to system maintenance ([`../16-second-order/macro.md`](../16-second-order/macro.md)) |

Secondary windows: a chaebol-scale succession scandal (Korea's recurring trigger — [korea-japan.md](korea-japan.md)); a constitutional-court strike-down forcing wholesale redesign (Austria 2008's mechanism, which could as easily force an upgrade as an abolition — [continental-europe.md](continental-europe.md)); and an OECD/G20 coordination round following the Pillar Two precedent ([`../08-capital-flight/international-coordination.md`](../08-capital-flight/international-coordination.md)).

## The Friedman playbook

Friedman's full formulation — "Only a crisis, actual or perceived, produces real change... Our basic function [is] to develop alternatives to existing policies, to keep them alive and available until the politically impossible becomes the politically inevitable" — was executed by his own movement across 1947–1980, and the same method built the 2001 US estate-tax repeal ([`../10-opposition/attack-playbook.md`](../10-opposition/attack-playbook.md)). Symmetry demands the levelling side run it too. Requirements before the window, mapped to this repo:

1. **A complete, costed, drafted design** with every relief, valuation rule, and anti-avoidance provision specified — [`../02-proposal/`](../02-proposal/README.md) and [`../07-implementation/`](../07-implementation/README.md) are that artefact in embryo; the gap is legislative drafting ([`../15-transition/legislative-path-uk.md`](../15-transition/legislative-path-uk.md), [`../15-transition/legislative-path-us.md`](../15-transition/legislative-path-us.md)).
2. **Pre-answered objections** so the 72-hour media contest after announcement is survivable — [`../09-objections/`](../09-objections/README.md), [`../14-communications/inoculation.md`](../14-communications/inoculation.md).
3. **A standing advocacy infrastructure** that can recognise the window and move — [`../13-movement/theory-of-change.md`](../13-movement/theory-of-change.md), [`../12-political-adoption/policy-entrepreneurs.md`](../12-political-adoption/policy-entrepreneurs.md).
4. **Geographic targeting.** Windows open locally: watch the UK budget cycle, Korean succession politics, and Dutch coalition formations first, per the ranking in [README.md](README.md).

## Analytic judgement

The realistic adoption scenario is conjunctural: window #1 (wealth-transfer salience) and #2 (housing electorate) are slow, certain tides that raise baseline support, while #3–#5 supply the discrete shock that converts support into legislation. No identified window *requires* a Scheidel-scale catastrophe — but absent any shock at all, the honest expectation is stepping-stone convergence (receipts-basis reforms, baby bonds, threshold politics — [`../12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md`](../12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md)) rather than the full 100% design. The manifesto's function, on this view, is exactly Friedman's: keep the idea alive, specified, and lying around.
