# France: The Machinery Exists, the Ideas Exist, the Politics Refuses

**Executive summary.** France is the only country where every intellectual and administrative component of this proposal already exists in national institutions — a recipient-based, lifetime-cumulating inheritance tax administered through a compulsory notarial filing system; forced heirship that has limited testamentary freedom since the Revolution; an official advisory council that has proposed lifetime-receipts taxation plus a universal capital guarantee; and a major party whose standing programme contains a literal maximum inheritance with everything above it redistributed to the young. It is simultaneously the country where the proposal faces the most fully assembled resistance: a constitutional court with an explicit "confiscatory taxation" doctrine, a Senate that reliably kills wealth-taxation bills, Europe's most hostile inheritance-tax polling, and a €2.1 trillion assurance-vie industry whose business model is partly an inheritance-tax shelter. France is the deep case for both halves of this project's thesis — the design is buildable today, and design alone adopts nothing.

**Key findings**

- **France already runs per-recipient taxation with temporal cumulation.** Droits de succession are assessed per heir, graded by kinship (45% top rate in the direct line, 60% between non-relatives), with a €100,000 per-child allowance [1] and a 15-year *rappel fiscal* that adds back prior gifts between the same donor and recipient before recomputing allowances and brackets [2][3]. This is the proposal's ledger logic — recipient basis plus lifetime-window cumulation — operating at national scale for decades.
- **A ready-made register infrastructure exists.** Every succession must be declared to the tax administration within 6 months of death (12 months for deaths abroad), with filing waived only below €50,000 in the direct line [4]; 17,306 notaires drafting ~4 million authentic acts a year already prepare and file these déclarations whenever real property passes [4][5]. No other candidate country has a professionalised, compulsory, per-succession reporting pipeline this complete.
- **The state limiting inheritance is ancient French law.** The *réserve héréditaire* compels parents to leave children fixed shares — half of the estate for one child, two-thirds for two, three-quarters for three or more [6] — a Civil Code descendant of the Revolution's equal-partition decrees ([`../05-history/has-this-been-proposed.md`](../05-history/has-this-been-proposed.md) §3). France has never treated bequest as an absolute private right.
- **The major finding: a literal 100%-above-a-cap proposal sits in a major party's programme.** La France insoumise's *L'Avenir en commun* proposes to "créer un héritage maximal de 12 millions d'euros (soit 100 fois le patrimoine net médian)" — a maximum inheritance of €12m, everything above it taken — alongside higher succession taxes on the largest estates computed "taking into account all gifts and inheritances received over the whole of life," i.e. a lifetime-receipts basis, with proceeds framed as funding a youth autonomy guarantee [7][8]. A cap-plus-youth-funding package has been on the platform of France's largest left party since January 2022.
- **Official-advisory endorsement is unmatched anywhere.** CAE Note 69 (*Repenser l'héritage*, 2021 — Dherbécourt, Fack, Landais, Stantcheva) proposed taxing lifetime inheritance flows received, closing the assurance-vie/Dutreil/dismemberment exemptions (€12–19bn), and a "garantie en capital pour tous" of up to €40,000 at 18 [9][10]; Piketty's €120,000 endowment at 25 is the design's most famous statement ([`../05-history/piketty.md`](../05-history/piketty.md)); the Blanchard–Tirole commission recommended the lifetime-recipient basis [11].
- **The resistance is equally assembled.** The Conseil constitutionnel's confiscatory-taxation doctrine (2012 censure of the 75% income-tax tranche [12]); the Senate, which killed the Zucman 2% wealth floor 188–129 on 12 June 2025 after the Assembly had passed it in February [13][14]; polling in which 77% call the inheritance tax unjustified and 84% want it cut — 79% still opposed to increases *after* being told most estates pay nothing [15]; and the assurance-vie shelter (€152,500 per beneficiary tax-free outside the succession, on a €2,107bn asset pool) defended by France's largest financial industry [16][17].
- **Verdict:** strongest intellectual infrastructure and best administrative fit in the world; blocked short-term by constitutional doctrine, the Senate, and the loophole lobbies. The door is the endowment-first framing the CAE itself supplied — *garantie de capital* — not the tax-first framing that has made the existing tax Europe's most hated.

## 2. The machinery half-exists

The standard objection to this proposal — that a per-recipient lifetime ledger is administratively fantastical ([`../09-objections/practicality.md`](../09-objections/practicality.md)) — fails in France even harder than in Ireland:

- **Recipient basis is current law.** French droits de succession are computed per heir, on each heir's share, at rates graded by kinship: after a €100,000 allowance per child, the direct-line schedule rises to 45% above €1,805,677; siblings pay 35–45%, relatives to the fourth degree 55%, and unrelated recipients 60% [1].
- **Temporal cumulation is current law.** The *rappel fiscal* (art. 784 CGI) adds back all gifts made by the same donor to the same recipient within the previous 15 years before applying allowances and bracket progression — allowances and low brackets reconstitute only after 15 gift-free years [2][3]. France already operates the proposal's core anti-avoidance mechanic: gifts and inheritances draw on one cumulating account. The reform ask is arithmetic, not conceptual: extend the window from 15 years to a lifetime, cumulate across *all* donors rather than per donor, and collapse the kinship grades into one universal threshold.
- **The register infrastructure exists.** A déclaration de succession must be filed with the tax administration within 6 months of a death in France (12 months abroad); only estates under €50,000 passing to direct-line heirs or the spouse (€3,000 for others) are excused [4]. Where the estate includes real property — the bulk of French wealth — a notaire's involvement is compulsory, and in practice the notaire prepares, signs and files the declaration [4]. The notariat is a state-regulated profession of 17,306 officers producing ~4 million authentic acts a year [5]: a compulsory, professionalised, per-succession reporting pipeline that the UK's Central Register would have to build from scratch.

The CAE said exactly this in 2021: its reform is built on "taxing the entirety of sums received by a person over their lifetime" — presented not as a new machine but as a re-basing of the machine France already runs [9][10].

## 3. The state has always limited inheritance here

The *réserve héréditaire* makes a fixed share of every estate the children's by right: half for one child, two-thirds for two, three-quarters for three or more; only the remaining *quotité disponible* is the testator's to direct [6]. This is Civil Code law descended directly from the Revolution's succession decrees — primogeniture abolished 1790–91, compulsory equal partition among children under the law of 17 Nivôse An II ([`../05-history/has-this-been-proposed.md`](../05-history/has-this-been-proposed.md) §3, repo-verified there). Two campaign consequences:

1. **"The state has no business in bequest" is not available to French opponents.** French law has told every parent for two centuries how much of their estate they may freely direct. A lifetime-receipts threshold is a *milder* intrusion on testamentary freedom than the réserve: it constrains only the amount any one recipient may take, not to whom the estate must go.
2. **The grain runs both ways.** The same réserve embeds inheritance as a family *right* — the child's protected share is the cultural default the proposal must reframe, which is why [continental-europe.md](continental-europe.md) lists it as a constraint. The honest reading: France's legal culture accepts state-structured inheritance while its political culture defends family transmission. The design question is which instinct a campaign activates ([`../06-palatability/framing.md`](../06-palatability/framing.md)).

The LFI plank deserves its precise weight. It is not a manifesto aside: the programme text creates a *maximum inheritance* — a cap, with the excess taken — pegged explicitly to 100 times median net wealth, paired with lifetime-receipts computation and a youth-funding narrative ("héritage maximum de 12 millions d'euros... pour financer la garantie d'autonomie" [8]). That is C1+C3+C5+C7 of the repo's component matrix — a confiscatory cap on a lifetime-receipts base with a youth transfer — proposed by a party that took the largest left vote share in 2022 and anchors the NFP bloc. The differences from this project are calibration and framing: LFI's cap is set at the 0.01% (€12m) rather than ~P90 (£500k); its youth transfer is an income (€1,063/month) rather than a capital endowment; and it leads with the cap, not the benefit. For the precedents file, the finding is blunt: **a top-five party in a G7 country currently proposes a literal 100%-above-a-threshold inheritance regime.** This belongs in [`../05-history/has-this-been-proposed.md`](../05-history/has-this-been-proposed.md)'s live-politics column and in [`../12-political-adoption/target-politicians.md`](../12-political-adoption/target-politicians.md)'s map.

## 5. The resistance case

### The polling paradox

France exhibits the sharpest version anywhere of the gap this project's palatability section exists to close. Odoxa (April 2024): 77% of the French find the inheritance tax unjustified; 84% want it reduced (13% raised); and — the killer detail — 79% still oppose increases *after being told* that most successions pay no tax at all [15]. Les Echos-reported 2023 polling found nearly three-quarters saying the duties are too high (repo-verified in [continental-europe.md](continental-europe.md)). The same electorate votes for parties committed to taxing the rich and passed a billionaire wealth floor through its lower house [14]. The CAE's own diagnosis is that the tax is as *misunderstood* as it is hated — citizens massively overestimate what ordinary families pay [9]. France is the standing proof that high rates on low thresholds with no visible benefit produce resentment rather than egalitarian consolidation — the design error the £500k threshold and the endowment exist to avoid ([`../06-palatability/polling.md`](../06-palatability/polling.md), [`../06-palatability/framing.md`](../06-palatability/framing.md)).

### The constitutional gate

The Conseil constitutionnel censured the 2012 75% income-tax tranche for imposing a burden contrary to equality before public charges (décision 2012-662 DC [12]) and polices a "caractère confiscatoire" boundary across wealth-related levies. A naïvely drafted 100% marginal rate invites the same fate. The drafting case for survival — **(judgement, flagged for French-counsel review)**: (i) the doctrine assesses the burden against *ability to pay*, and a recipient of more than €500k+ in lifetime windfalls has, by construction, received the means to pay from the very flow taxed; (ii) the *effective* rate on total lifetime receipts stays far below 100% — a recipient of €1m at a £500k-equivalent threshold keeps 50%+, and the marginal-100% structure is arithmetically an *héritage maximal*, a cap on receipts rather than a levy on property; (iii) the Conseil has never reviewed a cap-styled succession regime, and France's own réserve héréditaire shows quantity-limits on transmission are constitutionally native [6]. Against this: the Conseil looked precisely at *marginal* combined rates in 2012, and 100% is the number the doctrine exists to catch. Treat C9 = 2 as genuinely uncertain either way — unlike Germany's Art. 14, this is doctrine, not constitutional text, and doctrine moves ([`../10-opposition/legal-challenges.md`](../10-opposition/legal-challenges.md)).

### The Senate and the party map

The Zucman episode is the clean natural experiment: Assembly 116–39 in favour (February 2025, Éva Sas/Écologistes) [14]; Senate 188–129 against (12 June 2025), on capital-flight, investment-signal and constitutionality arguments [13]; Assembly rejection of the reintroduced versions in the autumn 2025 budget [18]; Senate rejection again in the 2026 budget round [13]. The indirectly elected Senate, structurally right-of-centre, is a standing veto on wealth taxation that no single election removes. The party arithmetic: LFI/Écologistes/PCF for (with PS favouring receipts-progressive reform on the Hidalgo pattern [20]); the Macron bloc committed to the *narrative de l'entrepreneur* and to gift-liberalisation (the 2025 loi de finances created a new €100k-per-donor / €300k-per-donee exemption for family gifts toward a primary residence [21]); LR and RN committed to *cutting* — Le Pen's 2022 programme would remove €300,000 of real property from the succession base and shorten the rappel from 15 to 10 years [22]; Macron's 2022 platform promised allowance rises too [23]. Direction of travel in actual legislation is toward *narrowing* the base, not broadening it.

## 6. The path, if there is one

The endowment-first sequence, translated ([`../12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md`](../12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md)):

1. **Lead with the *garantie de capital*.** France's own advisory council coined the instrument — up to €40,000 at 18, funded by exemption-closing [9][10]. A French campaign leads with the CAE's words, not with "impôt": the offer is *un capital de départ pour chaque jeune Français*, paid for by the assurance-vie and Dutreil niches that currently exempt the largest transmissions.
2. **Re-base before re-rating.** Lifetime-receipts conversion (rappel extended from 15 years to life, cumulated across donors, one universal allowance replacing the kinship grades) is Blanchard–Tirole/CAE orthodoxy [9][11] and can be sold as *lowering* taxes for the 87% of estates the CAE shows pay little or nothing — the Hidalgo €300k framing shows the left can campaign threshold-generous [20].
3. **Close the niches with the industry inside the tent.** Assurance-vie integration into the receipts base, grandfathered for existing contracts, is the revenue core (€12–19bn [9]) and the hardest fight; Dutreil converts to deferral.
4. **Rate escalation waits for the constituency.** The 100% tranche — an *héritage maximal*, LFI's word — is drafted as a cap with a generous threshold, presented to the Conseil constitutionnel with the effective-rate arithmetic of §5, and only after the endowment cohort exists. Constitutional pre-clearance work is the workstream France uniquely requires ([`../10-opposition/legal-challenges.md`](../10-opposition/legal-challenges.md)).
5. **The EU multiplier.** A converted France is the natural co-sponsor of the enhanced-cooperation floor in [continental-europe.md](continental-europe.md) — Paris-plus-Berlin remains the only geometry that solves the continental exit problem ([`../08-capital-flight/international-coordination.md`](../08-capital-flight/international-coordination.md)).

## 7. Analytic judgement

France is the inverse of the UK case. Britain has the political runway and must import the ideas; France has the ideas — in its advisory council, its most famous economist, and a major party's programme — and the world's best administrative substrate, but no legislative path while the Senate stands, the Conseil's doctrine is untested against a cap-styled draft, and the electorate hates the existing tax more than any in Europe. The campaign inference: France is not a first-mover bet but the movement's *arsenal* — the CAE note, the LFI precedent, the notarial proof-of-machinery, and the polling cautionary tale are exhibits every other country's campaign should deploy. Domestically, the only live door is the one the CAE opened: *garantie de capital* first, niches second, rate last. If a left government with Senate arithmetic ever coincides with a budget crisis and a drafted-to-survive cap, France could move faster than any country in this section — the machinery is already running.

## Honest limits

- The Conseil constitutionnel survival analysis in §5–6 is analyst judgement, not legal opinion; no French public-law review has been commissioned, and no cap-styled succession statute has ever been tested.
- LFI programme parameters are taken from the party's own programme site and movement press [7][8]; the €12m cap's enforcement drafting (tax vs civil-law cap) is not specified in the programme text itself.
- The claim that notaires file "the great majority" of déclarations is inferred from the compulsory-notaire rule for estates containing real property [4]; no official share-of-filings statistic was located.
- METI's specific lobbying record on Dutreil, FNSEA mobilisation capacity, and the notariat's likely posture are qualitative judgements, labelled as such.
- Reported early-2025 remarks by then-finance-minister Éric Lombard favouring heavier taxation of top transmissions could not be verified against a fetchable primary source and are omitted.
- The 2026 budget's final treatment of succession provisions post-dates the last verified source here; re-verify before manifesto use.
