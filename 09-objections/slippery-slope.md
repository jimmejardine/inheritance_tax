# Slippery-Slope Objections

**Executive summary.** Slope objections — "your house is next," "this is communism," "the register becomes a wealth tax" — are arguments about trajectory rather than content, so the answers must supply what the objections omit: a mechanism. In every case the political mechanism runs the other way — the proposal makes 90%+ of voters property-holders with a direct stake in the threshold, and its intellectual lineage is liberal-capitalist from Paine to Carnegie to Roosevelt.

Rules of engagement: concede, one-liner, full answer. Master table in [README.md](README.md).

## "First inheritance, next they'll take your house"

**Concede first.** Taxes do creep: UK income tax arrived in 1799 as a temporary war measure, and fiscal-drag stealth expansion is a real, documented phenomenon — including in the inheritance-tax history this project itself documents ([`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md), the Swedish threshold-drift case).

**One-line answer.** A slope argument needs a mechanism, and every mechanism here points the other way: the threshold is indexed to the *higher* of wages and house prices so it cannot drift downward in real terms, and after the endowment exists, cutting the threshold means facing tens of millions of voters who are net beneficiaries of the scheme as designed.

**Full answer.** Distinguish drift (silent erosion) from expansion (deliberate broadening):

- **Drift is engineered out.** Max-of-two indexation was chosen precisely because nominal-threshold decay is how the Swedish tax ended up taxing ordinary flats ([`../02-proposal/thresholds.md`](../02-proposal/thresholds.md)).
- **Expansion must be voted in the open.** Taking the allowance below £500k requires a parliamentary majority to act against an electorate in which the overwhelming majority inherit less than that and *receive* a £50k endowment funded by the current settings.
- **History says the live risk is repeal, not expansion** ([`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md)) — which is why the design entrenches the threshold formula and pairs the tax with a visible universal benefit.
- **Entrenchment options** (super-majority locks, sunset-protected indexation) belong in the legislative design ([`../15-transition/legislative-path-uk.md`](../15-transition/legislative-path-uk.md), [`../15-transition/grandfathering.md`](../15-transition/grandfathering.md)).

## "This is communism"

**Concede first.** A 100% marginal rate on anything is a strong state intervention, and the instinct to check any policy's direction of travel against the totalitarian record is healthy, not paranoid.

**One-line answer.** Communism abolishes private property; this proposal *universalises* it — every 25-year-old becomes a capital owner — and its intellectual lineage is Thomas Paine, John Stuart Mill, Andrew Carnegie, and Theodore Roosevelt, a list containing no communists and two of capitalism's most effective defenders.

**Full answer.** Check the proposal against what communism actually entails:

| Communism requires | This proposal |
|---|---|
| State ownership of the means of production | Absent — firms, markets, and prices untouched |
| Abolition of private property | Inverted — the endowment gives every citizen private capital: the "property-owning democracy" of Meade and Rawls |
| Central planning | Absent — the state is a pipe from taxed bequests to citizen grants ([`../02-proposal/endowment.md`](../02-proposal/endowment.md)) |
| Confiscation of lifetime earnings | Absent — 0% on everything anyone earns, holds, or spends while alive |

What remains is a rule about *transmission at death*, a domain the state has always regulated — primogeniture, entail, intestacy, the existing 40% IHT. The lineage matters rhetorically: Carnegie wrote that "the man who dies thus rich dies disgraced" and endorsed steep death duties in [*The Gospel of Wealth*](https://www.carnegie.org/about/our-history/gospelofwealth/) ([`../05-history/carnegie-1889.md`](../05-history/carnegie-1889.md)); Roosevelt, a Republican, proposed graduated inheritance taxation in 1906 to protect market capitalism from dynastic corrosion ([`../05-history/roosevelt-1906.md`](../05-history/roosevelt-1906.md)).

The strongest one-liner in debate: *communists want the state to own the capital; we want your kids to own some.*

## "The state owns your children's future"

**Concede first.** No state should direct anyone's life course, and a policy that made young adults' prospects depend on bureaucratic discretion would deserve exactly this attack.

**One-line answer.** The endowment is unconditional cash with no strings, no application, and no official deciding anything — whereas under the status quo it is *parental wealth* that owns children's futures, generously for a few and with empty hands for most.

**Full answer.** Trace who controls what under each regime:

- **Under this proposal:** at 25, every citizen receives £50,000 to use entirely as they choose — training, a deposit, a business, or (unwisely but freely) nothing durable at all; unconditionality is a deliberate design choice against paternalism ([`../02-proposal/endowment.md`](../02-proposal/endowment.md)). The state's role ends at payment.
- **Under the status quo:** a child's capital endowment at 25 is determined by parental wealth and parental favour — a private government from which there is no appeal, which the heirs-burden literature shows can function as an instrument of control over adult children's careers, partners, and choices ([`../03-case-for/heirs-burden.md`](../03-case-for/heirs-burden.md)).
- **For the majority who inherit nothing,** the "future" currently on offer is rent and wage-dependence in a housing market priced by other people's inheritances ([`../01-problem/housing.md`](../01-problem/housing.md), [`../01-problem/mobility.md`](../01-problem/mobility.md)).

The liberty argument, honestly weighed, favours the reform — and its residue is steelmanned in [`../04-case-against/liberty-autonomy.md`](../04-case-against/liberty-autonomy.md).

## "Once the register exists, it'll be used for a wealth tax"

**Concede first.** Information infrastructure is dual-use, function creep is a documented pattern in state data systems, and a Central Register of Receipts is a new government dataset that deserves scrutiny on exactly these grounds.

**One-line answer.** The register records *transfers received*, not *wealth held* — it cannot support an annual wealth tax without exactly the new valuation machinery and new legislation a wealth tax would need anyway — and by that standard PAYE, the Land Registry, Companies House, and CRS data are all already "wealth-tax infrastructure."

**Full answer.** This slope needs the register to be a *shortcut* to a wealth tax, and it is not:

- **A receipts ledger records flow events** — who received what, when, at what assessed value ([`../02-proposal/mechanism.md`](../02-proposal/mechanism.md)).
- **An annual wealth tax requires a stock census** — every asset, every person, every year — which is precisely the crushing valuation burden that distinguishes the two instruments ([whataboutery.md](whataboutery.md#why-not-a-wealth-tax-instead)).
- **Parliament could legislate a wealth tax today** with no receipts register, using the Land Registry and CRS feeds it already has. The marginal creep-enablement is close to nil.
- **The privacy exposure is bounded:** the register concerns the small minority receiving above-threshold transfers, under the same taxpayer-confidentiality law as all HMRC data.
- Data-governance specifics and the legal-challenge surface belong to [`../07-implementation/administration.md`](../07-implementation/administration.md) and [`../10-opposition/legal-challenges.md`](../10-opposition/legal-challenges.md).

## The general anatomy of a slope argument

Worth internalising for debate:

- A slippery-slope claim is only as good as its *mechanism* — the causal story by which step one produces step two against the wishes of the people taking step one. "Taxes have grown before" is a base rate, not a mechanism.
- When the objector supplies a mechanism, engage it on the merits (as done above for drift and for the register).
- When they do not, name the missing piece and supply the counter-mechanism: this policy manufactures tens of millions of small capital-holders with a personal stake in the threshold staying high — the most slope-resistant constituency it is possible to build.
