# Overton Strategies: Mainstreaming a "Radical" Idea

**Executive summary.** Ideas move from unthinkable to policy along a describable path — academic legitimacy first, elite discussability second, political safety last — and the 2010s mainstreaming of UBI shows the route works within a decade. This file sets out the model, its honest limitations, the respectability cascade for Universal Inheritance, and the deliberate division of labour between the radical flagship (100%) and moderate stepping-stones.

## The Overton window — model and critics

Joseph Overton's model: at any moment, only a band of policies is politically speakable; politicians follow the window, they rarely move it; movement comes from actors outside electoral politics saying currently-unspeakable things. The standard ladder runs *unthinkable → radical → acceptable → sensible → popular → policy*.

**Honest limitations (we should not oversell the model):**

- It is a metaphor, not a mechanism — it describes movement without explaining what causes it. The causal work is done by concrete things: books, crises, validators, repetition.
- Windows can be moved *on you*: attention is symmetric, and raising an idea's salience also mobilises its opponents earlier ([10-opposition/attack-playbook.md](../10-opposition/attack-playbook.md)). This is why [inoculation](inoculation.md) must run ahead of salience.
- "Shift the window by saying extreme things" fails when the extreme voice is dismissible. Radicalism moves windows only when it comes with *credibility attached* — which is the entire logic of the cascade below.

## The respectability cascade

The idea must ascend a ladder where each rung licenses the next. Skipping rungs — mass campaigning before elite legitimacy — produces a salience spike the opposition wins.

| Rung | Artefact | Licenses | Status / owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Academic papers | Peer-reviewed work on recipient-based lifetime taxation and capital endowments | Think tanks to cite without career risk | Lineage already exists: Atkinson, Piketty ([05-history/atkinson.md](../05-history/atkinson.md), [05-history/piketty.md](../05-history/piketty.md)) |
| 2. Think-tank reports | Costed UK proposal with carve-out design | Broadsheets to cover as news, not opinion | Resolution Foundation/IPPR adjacent work exists (to verify current state); full version needed |
| 3. Broadsheet columns | Economist- and conservative-authored op-eds ([channels.md](channels.md)) | Editors and broadcasters to book the topic | Sequencing: FT/Times before Guardian |
| 4. Select-committee evidence | Written and oral evidence to Treasury Committee inquiries on wealth taxation | MPs to engage without proposing | Requires rungs 1–2 as citations |
| 5. Backbench adoption | PMQs mentions, ten-minute-rule bills, APPG formation | Frontbench teams to commission internal costings | [12-political-adoption/party-incentives.md](../12-political-adoption/party-incentives.md) |
| 6. Manifesto | A stepping-stone variant in a major-party manifesto | Legislation ([15-transition/legislative-path-uk.md](../15-transition/legislative-path-uk.md)) | End state; see [12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md](../12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md) |

The cascade's currency is *citability*: each rung exists so the next rung's actor has something respectable to point at. This is the manifesto website's core function — the permanent, linkable proof-of-depth layer ([channels.md](channels.md)).

## The worked example: UBI's 2010s mainstreaming

In 2010 basic income was a seminar curiosity; by 2020 it had national pilots (Finland 2017–18), a US presidential campaign built on it (Yang 2020), major-party flirtations, and pandemic-era near-implementations. The mechanism, in order:

1. **A book wave** re-legitimised the idea intellectually (Standing's precariat work; Bregman's *Utopia for Realists*) — rung 1–2 material.
2. **Tech validators** — Silicon Valley figures endorsing pilots (Y Combinator's study) — gave it cross-ideological cover: hard to dismiss as left utopianism when funded by venture capitalists.
3. **Pilots** converted the debate from "should we?" to "what happened when they did?" — empiricism as respectability.
4. **A concrete number** ("$1,000 a month") did the retail work; nobody chanted the phrase "basic income guarantee."

Every element maps directly: Piketty is our book wave's foundation; founder-class validators are recruitable via the [Founder and the Heir narrative](narratives.md); endowment pilots are specifiable ([15-transition/pilots.md](../15-transition/pilots.md)); "£50k at 25" is our $1,000-a-month. UBI also supplies the cautionary lesson: it mainstreamed *discussion* without securing *policy*, partly because its cost story was never resolved. Universal Inheritance has the structural advantage there — it arrives with its funding mechanism attached.

## The radical flagship and the anchoring dividend

The deliberately provocative "100%" serves a specific cognitive function: **anchoring**. Negotiation and judgement research (Tversky & Kahneman's anchoring effects; to verify applied-politics literature) shows first numbers drag subsequent judgements toward them. While "100% above £500k" is the debated flagship, an 80–90% top-rate variant — or Piketty's graduated schedule — reads as the *moderate compromise* rather than as confiscation. Compare: absent the flagship, even restoring the UK's historical ~80% top estate rate ([05-history/uk-estate-duty.md](../05-history/uk-estate-duty.md)) would itself be the "extreme" pole of the debate.

**When to be radical vs when to be moderate:**

| Context | Register | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Books, academic debate, op-ed provocations | Flagship (100%) | Overton work; honesty premium; sets the anchor |
| Broadcast, doorstep, retail politics | Retail name + endowment ([naming-and-language.md](naming-and-language.md)) | Electoral work; the tax rate need never lead |
| Legislative negotiation | Stepping-stones ([12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md](../12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md)) | Take the 80% deal; the flagship remains the movement's north star |
| Coalition-partner briefings | Full candour on both registers | Allies must never be surprised by the flagship ([13-movement/coalitions.md](../13-movement/coalitions.md)) |

The discipline: the flagship is a *position*, the stepping-stone is a *transaction*, and spokespeople never let opponents collapse the two ("so you secretly want 100%?" — "I want every 25-year-old to get £50,000; I'll take any honest route there").

## Failure modes to avoid

- **Premature salience:** mass attention before rungs 1–3 exist hands the definition war to the opposition ([inoculation.md](inoculation.md) must precede scale).
- **Respectability capture:** the cascade sanding the idea down to a forgettable IHT tweak — the flagship's other job is preventing this.
- **Single-party capture:** once the idea codes as one party's property, half the window closes ([12-political-adoption/wedge-dynamics.md](../12-political-adoption/wedge-dynamics.md)). The Carnegie–Roosevelt lineage is the standing defence.
